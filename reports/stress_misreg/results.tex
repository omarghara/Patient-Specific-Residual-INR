\section*{Setup}
A well-registered follow-up slice is reconstructed from 50\% of its pixels (random). The prior is deliberately shifted by $s$ pixels before use, so the \emph{only} source of prior error is misregistration. A method that over-trusts the prior ghosts the shifted anatomy into unobserved regions. \textbf{current-only INR} uses no prior (robust floor); \textbf{prior-finetune} is NeRP-style; \textbf{ours} adds a frozen prior; \textbf{ours+gate} adds a spatial trust gate $g(c)\in[0,1]$ that opens where the prior conflicts with the data.
